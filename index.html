ABOUT INDY CTF
A welcoming introduction to cybersecurity

Welcome to INDY CTF, a beginner-friendly Capture The Flag competition hosted at Independence High School! This event is designed specifically for students who are curious about cybersecurity - no prior experience needed. Whether you've never coded before or you're already familiar with security concepts, there's a place for you here.

We know that CTFs can seem intimidating, but it doesn't have to be! A CTF is essentially a puzzle-solving competition where you complete challenges to find hidden "flags" (special codes). You'll learn by doing, with challenges designed to teach you as you go.

Our mission is simple: create a safe environment where everyone, especially beginners, can explore CTFs without fear. You'll be surprised what you can learn in just competition!

THE CHALLENGES

Our challenges are designed with you in mind. Each category includes easy introductory challenges that teach you the fundamentals before moving to harder ones. You don't need to know everything - just pick what interests you and start learning!

Cryptography: Decode secret messages using ciphers and patterns
Web Exploitation: Find hidden pages and security issues in websites
Binary Exploitation: Understand how programs work under the hood
Reverse Engineering: Take apart programs to see what they do
Forensics: Be a digital detective - analyze files and uncover hidden information
OSINT: Use internet research skills to gather information

Every challenge includes hints, and there will be mentors available to help if you get stuck. The goal is learning, not perfection. Even solving one challenge means you've learned something new!

RULES & JUDGING
How scoring and rules work

Scoring System:

Each challenge is worth points based on its difficulty level
Harder challenges are worth more points
Some challenges may have dynamic scoring that adjusts based on solve count
First solves may earn bonus points

Competition Rules:

No attacking the competition infrastructure or other teams
Flag sharing and collaboration between teams is prohibited
Use of automated scanning tools must be reasonable and approved
Respect all participants and maintain good sportsmanship

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Common questions answered
► I've never done anything like this before. Is that okay?
That's MORE than okay - that's exactly who we want here! INDY CTF is specifically designed for beginners. You don't need any prior experience with cybersecurity, hacking, or even coding. We'll have intro challenges that teach you the basics, mentors to help you, and a friendly environment where questions are encouraged. Everyone starts somewhere, and this is a great place to start!
► What is a CTF?
A Capture The Flag (CTF) competition is a cybersecurity event where participants solve security-related challenges to find hidden "flags" (special strings of text). It's like a digital scavenger hunt that tests your hacking skills, problem-solving abilities, and knowledge of security concepts. But don't worry - we start with the absolute basics!
► What if I'm not good at math or programming?
You don't need to be! While some challenges involve problem-solving, many are more about creativity, research, and pattern recognition than math skills. Plus, there are different types of challenges - if programming isn't your thing, try forensics or OSINT challenges instead. And honestly, you might surprise yourself at what you can do when you try!
► What if I don't solve any challenges?
First off, you will solve at least one - we promise! We have challenges designed specifically for absolute beginners. But even if you're struggling, just attempting challenges means you're learning. Ask mentors for help, work with teammates, use hints - that's all part of the process. There's no shame in finding things difficult; that's how learning works.
► Do I need experience?
Nope! Zero experience required. Can you use Google? Can you follow instructions? Do you like puzzles? Then you have everything you need to start. The whole point of INDY CTF is to introduce students to cybersecurity in a welcoming way. We want you here regardless of your background!
► Is there a cost to participate?
No! INDY CTF is completely free to participate. There are no registration fees or costs. Just bring yourself, your laptop, and your enthusiasm for learning.
► Can I compete solo?
Yes! You can compete individually or form teams with other participants. Both solo players and teams are welcome. Teams typically work well because different members can focus on different challenge categories based on their strengths.
► What should I bring?
Bring your laptop (fully charged), power adapter, mouse if you prefer one, and a water bottle. Make sure you have a working internet connection capability. Everything else will be provided through our online platform.
► Can I use online resources?
Absolutely! CTFs encourage research and learning. You can use search engines, documentation, tutorials, and online tools to help solve challenges. However, you cannot share flags or solutions with other teams or ask others to solve challenges for you.
► Are there prizes?
Yes! INDY CTF will award prizes to top-performing individuals and teams. Beyond prizes, the real reward is the skills you'll develop, the knowledge you'll gain, and the experience you'll have competing.
► How long does the competition last?
The competition duration will be announced closer to the event date. Typically, CTF competitions run for several hours to a full day, giving you plenty of time to work through challenges at your own pace.
► What can I learn from participating?
You'll learn practical cybersecurity basics like how encryption works, how to find hidden information in files, and how websites can have security flaws. But beyond technical skills, you'll develop problem-solving abilities, learn to think creatively, and gain confidence in tackling unfamiliar challenges. Most importantly, you'll learn that cybersecurity isn't as scary as it seems - it's actually pretty interesting!
